30 Pain Points mapped to solutions, owners, and improvement phase
👤 People & Knowledge

PP1 — AMs lack end-to-end process understanding

PP2 — Hygiene checks skipped before raising tickets

PP3 — No standardised training material or knowledge base

PP4 — Knowledge sits with few individuals — single point of failure

Fixes: AM training programme (F1) · Expert/buddy rotation (F2) · Live SOP portal (S5) · Pre-ticket checklist in V2 (Q4)
⚙️ Process & Ownership

PP5 — No SOPs or TATs for common change requests

PP6 — OPD activation and rollout-support sit with separate teams; AMs don't know whom to call

PP7 — No single point of contact per corporate / change type

PP8 — Every minor change requires AM validation — impractical at scale

PP9 — Requests denied without explaining why or offering alternative

Fixes: SPOC matrix (Q1) · SOP + TAT for top 5 types (Q2) · Resolution-with-alternative template (F8) · Standing approvals matrix (F10) · Self-service admin tool (S1)
🤝 Communication & Coordination

PP10 — AMs liaise with multiple internal teams for a single change

PP11 — Multiple follow-ups required to move tasks forward

PP12 — Calls to Ops Enablement and Support frequently unanswered

PP13 — Some interactions perceived as unhelpful / rude

PP14 — Clients escalate to AM before AM is aware of the issue

Fixes: Single ticket entry point via SPOC (Q1) · Shared go-live tracker (Q8) · Defined response-time SLA (Q5) · Soft-skills refresher (F9) · Proactive monitoring dashboard (S4)
📋 Data Quality

PP15 — Initial member data is unfiltered / unsanitised

PP16 — Endorsement data updates consistently delayed

PP17 — Tickets raised without verifying DOB, email, MA IDs, duplicate employee IDs

PP18 — Clients propose solutions instead of stating problems

Fixes: Pre-ticket validation checklist in V2 (Q4) · Mandatory data-quality validation (Q3) · Pre-upload de-duplication script (F4) · Endorsement SLA with clients (F7) · Standard intake form forcing problem-statement format
🖥️ Tools & Systems

PP19 — Login creation in CMS delayed — 24-hr SLA not met

PP20 — Password reset is manual — absence of operator stalls go-live

PP21 — Test users often missing from CMS, blocking pre-go-live validation

PP22 — Features working in staging fail in production (drift)

PP23 — Last-minute domain changes break CAS/IWP/Auth sync

Fixes: Self-service password reset (Q6) · Auto-provision test users (F5) · Domain freeze T–7 + sync script (F6) · Production-parity framework (S2) · Automated login creation from CAS (S3)
📅 Planning & Prioritisation

PP24 — Requirements arrive at last minute after many iterations

PP25 — Teams not given advance notice of go-live dates

PP26 — Final go-live confirmation often missing

PP27 — Tasks tagged urgent without justification

PP28 — Tickets submitted in wrong service-desk bucket

Fixes: Go-live calendar published 2 weeks ahead (Q2) · Mandatory go-live sign-off ticket (Q10) · Urgency rubric + reviewer (Q9) · Dropdown-driven ticket form (F3) · Change-request cut-off T–5 days · Client-facing onboarding playbook (S6)

Headline Findings
~60% of delays trace to two root causes Lack of process ownership / SOPs and weak coordination across departments are the dominant structural drivers.
Data validation = highest-leverage quick win Pre-ticket validation of DOB, email, MA IDs, and duplicate checks would eliminate a recurring rework class costing days per rollout.
V2 template + SOP publish = single biggest lever A consolidated validated template and published SOP/TAT for the top five rollout types address the majority of reported pain points.
